Code Your World with Arduino

November 1st, 2014
EastPoint Tech
1:00pm - 5:00pm

An interactive programming workshop for middle school students using the Arduino prototyping kit.

Tell Me More!

Technologists of Color is a group committed to building the African American tech community in Atlanta. Our primary goal is to connect tech professionals so that they can share knowledge, resources and opportunities. We also strive to inform and educate the community to help increase African American participation in the rapidly growing world of technology.

We view the education and mentoring of our youth as a key to growing the number of African Americans in the tech space for years to come. The "Code Your World" series of workshops is an effort to provide this education. In the workshop, students will learn how they can use computers to interact with the physical world. By the end of class students will understand how many of the everyday things we encounter are programmable and think of new ways to program the world around them.


Goals and Objectives

    • Learn to build an electronic prototype using a sensor
    • Learn how sensors are programmed and used in the real world
    • Learn to work with others, as part of a team, to build functioning electronic prototypes

The Internet of Things...
Is the next BIG thing.

From automatically turning on and off lights to detecting temperature changes students will create projects using the Arduino prototyping kit that will demonstrate the basics of electronics programming and introduce the "IoT" concept.
